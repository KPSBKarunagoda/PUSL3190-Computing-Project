Understanding Phishing Attacks

Phishing is a type of social engineering attack often used to steal user data, including login credentials and credit card numbers. It occurs when an attacker, masquerading as a trusted entity, dupes a victim into opening an email, instant message, or text message.

PhishGuard helps you identify potential phishing attempts by analyzing URLs and websites for common indicators of phishing.

Understanding phishing attacks is the first step toward protecting yourself online. Below are the main types of phishing attacks you should be aware of.

Email Phishing

The most common type of phishing attack involving emails that appear to come from trusted sources but contain malicious links or attachments.

  • Impersonates legitimate companies
  • Often creates a sense of urgency
  • Contains suspicious links or attachments

Clone Phishing

Attackers create nearly identical copies of legitimate messages but replace links or attachments with malicious versions.

  • Looks identical to legitimate communications
  • Often claims to be a "resend" or "updated version"
  • May reference previous communications

Spear Phishing

Targeted attacks directed at specific individuals or companies, typically more personalized and harder to detect.

  • Contains personally relevant information
  • References colleagues, managers, or events
  • Highly customized and convincing

Whaling

A type of spear phishing aimed specifically at senior executives and other high-profile targets.

  • Highly sophisticated and well-researched
  • Often involves financial documents
  • May impersonate board members or partners

How to Identify Phishing Attempts

Phishing attacks can be sophisticated, but there are several red flags to watch for:

  • Suspicious sender email addresses - Check if the domain matches the company it claims to be from
  • Poor spelling and grammar - Legitimate organizations typically have high standards for their communications
  • Sense of urgency - Messages that push you to act quickly often aim to prevent you from thinking critically
  • Suspicious links - Hover over links to see their true destination before clicking
  • Unusual requests - Be wary of requests for sensitive information or unusual payment methods
  • Generic greetings - Legitimate companies usually address you by name

Always check the URL before entering sensitive information. Phishing URLs often look similar to legitimate ones but contain subtle differences or unusual characters.

URL Red Flags

  • Misspelled domain names - Like "amaz0n.com" or "paypa1.com"
  • Subdomains that try to trick you - "paypal.secure-payment.com" (where secure-payment.com is the actual domain)
  • IP addresses instead of domain names - Like http://123.45.67.89/...
  • URL shorteners - These can hide the true destination of a link
  • HTTP instead of HTTPS - Legitimate sites use secure connections for sensitive information

How to Protect Yourself

Take these steps to protect yourself from phishing attacks:

  1. Always verify the sender - Check that emails come from legitimate domains
  2. Think before you click - Hover over links to see where they lead
  3. Keep software updated - This includes your operating system, browsers, and security software
  4. Use multi-factor authentication - This adds an extra layer of security even if your password is compromised
  5. Be suspicious of unexpected attachments - Even from people you know
  6. Visit websites directly - Type the URL into your browser instead of clicking links
  7. Check for HTTPS - Look for the padlock icon in your browser
