What is Phishing?

Phishing is a type of cyber attack where attackers impersonate legitimate organizations through emails, websites, or messages to steal sensitive information such as usernames, passwords, credit card details, or other personal data.

Did You Know?

The term "phishing" is a play on the word "fishing," as attackers use bait (fraudulent communications) to hook victims and steal their information.

Common Characteristics of Phishing Attempts

  • Sense of Urgency - Messages that create a sense of urgency, claiming immediate action is required
  • Suspicious Links - URLs that look similar to legitimate websites but contain subtle misspellings
  • Poor Grammar - Grammatical errors, typos, or awkward phrasing in communications
  • Unsolicited Attachments - Unexpected email attachments that could contain malware
  • Requests for Sensitive Information - Asking for personal data, passwords, or financial details

Types of Phishing Attacks

Email Phishing

The most common form of phishing, where attackers send fraudulent emails impersonating reputable companies to steal personal information or login credentials.

Learn more about email phishing
Spear Phishing

Targeted attacks directed at specific individuals or companies, usually containing personalized information to make the messages more convincing.

Learn more about spear phishing
Website Spoofing

Creating fake websites that mimic legitimate ones, often with URLs that look similar but contain slight variations or misspellings.

Learn more about website spoofing

Protecting Yourself from Phishing

Essential Protection Strategies

  • Verify Sender Information - Always check the email address of the sender, not just the display name
  • Hover Before Clicking - Hover over links to see the actual URL before clicking
  • Use Multi-Factor Authentication - Enable MFA on all important accounts to add an extra layer of security
  • Keep Software Updated - Regularly update your operating system and applications to patch security vulnerabilities
  • Use PhishGuard's URL Analyzer - Check suspicious URLs before visiting them

Important Warning

Never share sensitive information like passwords, credit card details, or social security numbers via email, even if the request appears to come from a legitimate organization.

Suspicious elements

Did you know?

Social media phishing attacks have increased by 500% in recent years. Attackers often impersonate friends or create fake profiles of celebrities to trick users.

How to protect yourself:

  • Verify the identity of contacts through another channel if they send suspicious links
  • Be suspicious of new accounts or requests from people you're already friends with
  • Never download attachments from social media messages
  • Check the file extension - legitimate images won't end in .exe, .bat, or other executable formats
