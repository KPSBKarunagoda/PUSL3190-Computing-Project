How to Get Email Headers:

Gmail

  1. Open the email in Gmail
  2. Click on the three dots (⋮) in the upper right corner
  3. Select "Show original"
  4. You'll see the full headers - click "Copy to clipboard"
  5. Paste the headers into the text area above

Outlook

  1. Open the email in Outlook
  2. Click on the three dots (...) in the top right corner
  3. Select "View message details" or "View > Message details"
  4. A new window will open showing the email headers
  5. Copy all the text and paste it in the text area above

Apple Mail

  1. Open the email in Apple Mail
  2. Select "View" from the menu bar
  3. Hold the "Option" key and click "Show all message headers"
  4. The message will display with full headers
  5. Copy all the text and paste it in the text area above

Yahoo Mail

  1. Open the email in Yahoo Mail
  2. Click the three dots (...) in the top right of the message
  3. Select "View raw message"
  4. A new tab will open with the full headers
  5. Copy all the text and paste it in the text area above

How to Spot Email Phishing Attempts

1
Check the Sender
Look carefully at the sender's email address, not just the display name. Phishers often use addresses that look similar to legitimate companies but with small spelling differences.
2
Examine Authentication
Legitimate organizations use SPF, DKIM, and DMARC to verify their emails. Failed authentication can indicate spoofing attempts.
3
Watch for Unusual Routing
Emails that pass through multiple servers in different countries or use unexpected mail servers could indicate a phishing attempt.
4
Check for Urgency
Phishing emails often create a false sense of urgency, pushing you to act quickly without thinking. Be suspicious of urgent demands.
